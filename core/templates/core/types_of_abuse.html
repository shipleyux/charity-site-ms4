Understanding Domestic Abuse

Emotional abuse involves patterns of behaviour that chip away at a person’s self-esteem and confidence. It can include name-calling, manipulation, isolation from loved ones, or making the person feel worthless or dependent.

Physical abuse includes any act of violence such as hitting, slapping, pushing, or the use of weapons. It can also involve controlling behaviours like locking someone in a room or preventing them from getting medical help.

Sexual abuse involves any sexual activity without consent. This can include rape, unwanted touching, coercion, or being made to watch or perform sexual acts against one’s will.

Financial abuse includes controlling someone's access to money, stealing income or savings, placing debt in their name, or preventing them from working or accessing financial information.

Digital abuse involves using technology (phones, social media, GPS, etc.) to control, harass, stalk, or intimidate a partner. It can include monitoring activity, sending threats, or demanding constant contact.
